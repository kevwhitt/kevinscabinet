Design Principles in Mobile Sites

Proximity — Makenna Gunter

roolee.com

This site uses good proximity because everything is close to eachother but not to close you can tell that things are there own thing! It shows good organization and I think it makes good use of white space throughout the page!

Alignment — Matt Clayson

starwars.com

This website uses alignment to create a clean, unified look while also drawing the user's attention to the purpose of the site. That is, in this case, to sell tickets for the movie Rogue One.

Repetition — David Lino

www.wikipedia.org

In this website, we are able to find repetition in fonts, in hyperlinks, and in colors. Making use of this principle help us to have a better visualization and understanding of what is showed. For web developers, it helps organize the information they want to share.

Contrast — Ashley Nielson

https://www.bose.com/en_us/index.html

What is striking about this website is the contrast of the gray and white background and the dark or more colorful product allows the product to stand out and become the focus or your attention.

Typography — Kevin Whittaker

toms.com

I like the way that Toms uses their typography to make their mission statement stand out. The "One for One" slogan is written in a different style from everything else. And their font colors usually match the color of the product.
